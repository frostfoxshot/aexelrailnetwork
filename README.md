# Aexel Rail Network (ARN)
### *Bringing Real-World Train Safety to the Virtual Frontier*

The **Aexel Rail Network** is an open-source, high-integrity train protection system designed for virtual environments.
Inspired by the **European Train Control System (ETCS)**, ARN aims to standardize rail safety, prevent collisions, 
and provide a realistic "Driver Machine Interface" (DMI) for builders and operators.

---

## Mission Statement
The Aexel Rail Network plans to bring professional-grade train safety into the virtual world. 
By providing a unified protocol for trackside signaling and on-board automation, 
we aim to eliminate the "chaos" of Stormworks rail operations and replace it with a disciplined, realistic simulation.

## Features
* **Automatic Train Protection (ATP):** Real-time monitoring of speed vs. track limits.
* **Braking Curves:** Intelligent calculation of service and emergency braking distances.
* **Balise Simulation:** A standardized "Balise" (transmitter) system for track-to-train communication. -- Balise are not visual and are assumed by the system.
* **Modular DMI:** Realistic cockpit display (RCD).
* **Movement Authority:** Level 2 capability via radio-based block control for high-density traffic.

## Contribution & AI Policy
We believe that safety systems require human understanding and professional thoughts of virtual physics engines. :3

* **Human-Centric Coding:** Commits coded primarily with AI (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) **will not be accepted**. We value code that is understood and maintainable by its author.
* **The "Debugging" Exception:** AI is a tool, not an author. Using AI to find syntax errors, optimize mathematical formulas, or explain logic bottlenecks is permitted and encouraged.
* **Quality Control:** All contributions must be vigerously (i think thats how its spelt :p) tested to ensure low-latency performance in multiplayer environments.
